christopher j. joy

I'm principal consultant at mekong lab, a full-service economic research and risk consultancy working throughout Southeast Asia. I'm a researcher interested in developing economies with a particular focus on Southeast Asia's rapid growth and economic rise. I consider my work interdisciplinary, pulling from female empowerment and poverty reduction to state-society relations. I hold an MSc in Development Economics and an MA in Southeast Asian Studies from City, University of London and SOAS, Universty of of London respectively. I did my undergraduate work at the University of Pittsburgh

I am a former software developer at a Fortune 500 company. I recently returned to graduate school, diving into econometrics (Stata), Southeast Asia (particularly Vietnam, Inodnesia and Thailand), and economic modeling. I'm also fascinated by currencies, the role of the US dollar and the political economy of finance. I have spent significant portions of the last decade in Southeast Asia and speak Vietamese, Indonesian and Thai.

Papers

The Embedded State and Vietnam's COVID-19 Response
The paper argues Vietnam's ruling party's embedded state capacity marshalled political mobilization while altering dynamics of state-society relations in a retrenchment of earlier marketisation of life to contain COVID-19's spread.
Found Women: Economic Development and Female Empowerment in Southeast Asia
Against the backdrop of the world's “missing women”, Southeast Asia offers an example of both developed and developing countries that do not see unnatural sex-birth imbalance and raises the question, what is the state of female empowerment and economic development in Southeast Asia/ASEAN?
