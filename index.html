I'm Christopher -- a consultant and researcher interested in developing economies with a particular focus on Southeast Asia's rapid growth and economic rise. I consider my work interdisciplinary, pulling from such diverse areas as female empowerment, poverty reduction to the region's political economies and state-society relations. I also, on occaision, focus on international macroeconomics. I have spent significant portions of the last decade in Southeast Asia and speak Vietamese, Indonesian and Thai.

Working Life

I worked as a software and support developer in the private sector, for a at the time Fortune 500 company, where I developed and maintained software for the chemical industry. I also provided technical support to clients, trained users, and built documentation repositories. I then moved in roles at smaller and mid-sized companies, eventually starting my own company with a former colleague. After selling the company, I spent the next few years consulting for small and mid-sized businesses in the US and Southeast Asia prior to the COVID-19 pandemic. Following the sale of my company, I returned to graduate school to pursue my interests in economics and Southeast Asia.

Education

I hold an MSc in Development Economics and an MA in Southeast Asian Studies from City, University of London and SOAS, Universty of of London respectively. I did my undergraduate work at the University of Pittsburgh in Finance and Computer Science.

The Embedded State and Vietnam's COVID-19 Response
The paper argues Vietnam's ruling party's embedded state capacity marshalled political mobilization while altering dynamics of state-society relations in a retrenchment of earlier marketisation of life to contain COVID-19's spread.
Found Women: Economic Development and Female Empowerment in Southeast Asia
Against the backdrop of the world's “missing women”, Southeast Asia offers an example of both developed and developing countries that do not see unnatural sex-birth imbalance and raises the question, what is the state of female empowerment and economic development in Southeast Asia/ASEAN.
Private Equity, The Enabling State and The Logics of Infrastructure Investment
Using the lens of England's waterinfrastructure investment, the article argues that the early reverberations of privatisation, alongside enabling states, empowered large pools of private equity (PE) capital to rearrange asset logics and invest in public infrastructure
Extra-welfarism: Welfare Economics and The Expanded Childhood Tax Credit
Policy brief on the US's expanded Child Tax Credit (CTC) as part of the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021. The brief discusses the CTC in the context of welfare economics and extra-welfarism.
Nusantara: Ibukota Baru or Ghost Town?
Policy brief on Indonesia's new capital city, Nusantara. The brief discusses the political economy of the move, environmental concerns, and the economic rationale of the move.
